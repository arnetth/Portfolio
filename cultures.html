Email Developer Intern at LendingTree

May ‐ August 2021

As an email developer intern at LendingTree, I worked directly with the creative, marketing, and technical teams to produce fully-functioning, customer-facing emails that covered all the brands, products, and resources that the company has to offer.

I want to note that all the emails I developed except the Evergreen project were designed by LendingTree’s creative team. My day-to-day responsibilities involved working on Jira tickets, which included Zeplin files of email designs, subject lines and preheaders, and any other necessary information that I needed to know to develop the email. These emails had to not only look good, they had to work good. As a developer, I carefully completed a checklist of requirements for each email. They had to be developed for desktop, tablet, and mobile screen sizes for multiple browsers, and also had to be compatible with dark mode settings. Additionally, they had to be fully dynamic and personalized, in which the email’s content would change according to the user it is being sent out to, including the name, email heading, products, and even tables and images. Some emails also required external fetch and auto-login functions to log users directly into the MyLT app. I mostly used HTML, CSS, and Shopify Liquid to develop the emails.

Student Loan Hero Giveaway Emails

Dipping into email marketing

My Role

researcher
copywriter

Project Duration

2 weeks

Situation

Early on in my internship, LendingTree’s child company that the team develops emails for, Student Loan Hero, was holding a scholarship giveaway for any high school or college students who signed up using their email. The purpose of this giveaway was to increase the number of Student Loan Hero email subscribers. One of my coworkers on the email marketing side of the team ran the email campaign for this giveaway, and she reached out to me for help.

Task

She felt that the email subject lines and preheaders that they were using for promoting this giveaway were not were not attractive enough to grab the attention of subscribers, so she asked me to think of some creative subject lines and preheaders that we could use to freshen up this campaign.

Action

Starting off, I knew that I would need to understand what the campaign is doing already, and identify where it could improve. I looked at Student Loan Hero’s subject lines and preheaders to understand the company’s marketing appeal. I also looked at what its competitors are doing. I used companies like Niche and Fastweb to conduct some competitive research and analysis and used their emails as inspiration for my own ideas.

While brainstorming, I reflected on my own experiences receiving scholarship emails. As a tuition-paying college student, I am well versed with emails sent from companies like Student Loan Hero. Personalized subject lines tend to attract my attention more, especially if it mentions my school. Some of Student Loan Hero’s competitors did this. The giveaway campaign’s old subject lines and preheaders were not personalized, or sometimes just with the users’ first names. From this, my main objective was to create some unique, personalized subject lines and preheaders. I knew that we could use Shopify Liquid to accomplish this.

Evergreen Emails

Designer, Developer, Copywriter

My Role

Email designer

Email developer

Copywriter

Project Duration

5-6 weeks

Situation

The email team wanted to create a new set of emails to be used in customer communications journeys, which would direct them to LendingTree's free online articles and resources that are relevant to the customer's financial goals. For example, if a customer uses LendingTree to apply for a loan but they are rejected, we would want to send them an email for a LendingTree article about improving their credit score. We called these "evergreen" emails. The goal of this project was to increase customer engagement and improve our customer relationship management.

Task

I spearheaded this project from start to finish. I was given a list of specific articles and resources from the marketing team to design, develop, and write copy for.

Action

I designed, developed, and wrote the copy for all 35 "evergreen" emails.

The first step in creating these emails was to design one email template for all 35 "evergreen" emails to maintain consistency. We wanted the design to be minimalistic and simple, drawing all of the attention to the article or resource information. The hero copy and subject lines are the titles of the articles or resources. To allow a visual break from text, I wanted to include a fitting icon for each email in the hero section. The email copy highlights the main points of the article and always includes a call-to-action button at the bottom which links to the article or resource itself.

Results

This project was significant in my internship experience because I was given a lot of wiggle room to make my own design and development decisions. I was able to personally test my skills to identify my strengths and weaknesses.
